# Audit Service Pricing Model

## Cost structure
- **Direct operating cost:** **$0**
- **Workforce model:** AI agents act as the delivery workforce
- **Budget stance:** zero-budget operating model
- **Implication:** no labor, software, hosting, or tooling costs are assumed in this model

## Revenue model
The audit service is priced as a productized offer with clear entry and upsell paths:

| Offer | Price | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Basic audit | **$300** | Entry-level repository and GitHub features review |
| Audit + fix | **$1K–$3K** | Audit plus remediation and implementation support |
| Monthly retainer | **$1K–$5K/mo** | Ongoing monitoring, improvements, and advisory support |

## Break-even analysis
- **Break-even client count:** **1 client**
- **Reason:** cost is **$0**, so the first sale covers the full operating model

## 30-day forecast
Expected first-month mix:
- **3 clients × $300 basic audit = $900**
- **1 upsell × $1K audit + fix = $1K**
- **Total 30-day forecast: $1.9K**

## 90-day forecast
Expected quarter-end mix:
- **10 clients × $300 basic audit = $3K**
- **3 upsells × $1K–$3K audit + fix = $3K–$9K**
- **2 retainers × $1K/mo = $2K/mo**

## Margin
- **Gross margin: 100%**
- **Why:** zero direct cost against every dollar of service revenue

## Client ROI calculation
A basic audit priced at **$300** can be justified by avoided downside risk alone.

### Example ROI case
- **Client audit cost:** $300
- **Potential savings from preventing 1 security breach:** **$50K–$200K**
- **Net ROI multiple:**
  - Low case: **$50,000 / $300 = 166.7x**
  - High case: **$200,000 / $300 = 666.7x**

### ROI interpretation
Even a single prevented breach produces a return that is orders of magnitude above the audit fee, which makes the service an easy value proposition for risk-aware buyers.

## Positioning note
This model is built for speed, low overhead, and high-margin delivery. The business goal is to convert low-friction audits into higher-value remediation and recurring advisory work.
