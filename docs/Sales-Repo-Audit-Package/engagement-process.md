# Engagement Process — AI-SDLC Repo Audit

## Step 1: Client contacts Roman
The client reaches out to Roman with the repository URL, project context, and any specific concerns.

## Step 2: Roman sends the one-pager to the client
Roman shares the sales one-pager so the client understands the scope, deliverables, price, and turnaround time.

## Step 3: Client grants repository access
The client provides **read access** to the repository and any relevant supporting links, such as CI/CD dashboards or documentation.

## Step 4: Team executes the audit
The team performs the audit over **3 business days**, reviewing code quality, security, CI/CD, tests, documentation, and GitHub feature usage.

## Step 5: Team delivers the report to Roman
The team compiles the findings into a client-ready report and sends it to Roman for delivery.

## Step 6: Roman sends the report to the client
Roman delivers the audit report to the client and walks them through the key findings and recommendations.

## Step 7: Follow-up offer
If the client wants implementation support, Roman offers the **Audit+Fix Sprint** priced at **$1K-$3K** depending on the scope of fixes.

---

## Notes for the team
- Keep the report practical and executive-friendly
- Prioritize findings by impact and urgency
- Make recommendations specific enough for the client to act on immediately
- Highlight quick wins first, then deeper refactors
