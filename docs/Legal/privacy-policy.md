# Privacy Policy for GitHub Repository Audit Service

JAGM IT Company (“we,” “us,” or “our”) provides a GitHub repository audit service. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect information when a client uses that service.

## 1. Information we collect

To perform an audit, we may collect and process the following information:

- **Repository contents**: source code, documentation, configuration files, workflow files, commit history, issue content, pull request content, and other repository materials you provide or allow us to access.
- **Repository metadata**: repository name, owner or organization name, branch names, file paths, commit timestamps, contributor usernames, issue and pull request metadata, dependency manifests, and similar technical metadata.
- **Service communication data**: messages, instructions, and status updates related to the audit.
- **Billing and contact data**: client name, email address, invoice details, and payment status information needed to provide the service.

We collect only the information reasonably necessary to complete the audit and deliver the report.

## 2. How we use information

We use collected information only for the following purposes:

- to perform the requested audit analysis
- to identify security, quality, compliance, or process issues in the repository
- to prepare and deliver the audit report
- to communicate with the client about the audit
- to manage billing, accounting, and service administration

We do **not** use client repository data for marketing, resale, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to the audit service.

## 3. Confidentiality and data sharing

We treat client repository content and audit materials as confidential.

- We do **not** sell client data.
- We do **not** share client data with third parties except where required to provide the service, meet legal obligations, or support secure internal operations.
- Any internal access is limited to personnel who need the information to complete the audit or related administrative tasks.

## 4. Data retention

We retain audit data and related records for **30 days after audit delivery** unless a longer retention period is required by law, tax obligations, dispute handling, or client-requested follow-up work.

After the retention period, we will delete or anonymize the data in a reasonable manner unless we are legally required to keep it longer.

## 5. Client rights

Depending on applicable law, you may have the right to:

- access the personal data we hold about you
- request correction of inaccurate information
- request deletion of your personal data
- object to or restrict certain processing
- request a copy of your data in a portable format where applicable
- withdraw consent where processing is based on consent

To exercise these rights, contact us using the details below. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

## 6. Security measures

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards to protect client data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or loss. No system can be guaranteed to be fully secure, but we take confidentiality and data protection seriously.

## 7. International transfers

If client data is transferred or accessed across borders, we will handle it in a manner intended to preserve confidentiality and comply with applicable law.

## 8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The latest version will apply to future services and will be made available with the audit service materials.
